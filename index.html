Enhancing 6D Pose Estimation for Metallic Objects in Industrial Settings with Synthetic Datasets

Abstract

Monocular 6D pose estimation for metallic objects in industrial environments remains challenging due to the sim-to-real gap caused by low-fidelity synthetic images. In this paper, we present a novel dataset generation pipeline that synthesizes photorealistic images of metallic parts by explicitly modeling specular reflections under realistic lighting conditions and material properties to enhance pose estimation accuracy.

Our approach utilizes differentiable rendering to extract high-resolution metallic surface textures from multi-view images, preserving fine-grained surface details. To further bridge the sim-to-real gap, we employ a Vision Language Model (VLM) to parameterize the simulator from real-world images, replicating the real-world optical characteristics of metallic materials within the synthetic environment. %This enables the generation of datasets that accurately capture view-dependent reflections and specular highlights, which are critical for pose estimation on reflective surfaces. By aligning the simulated scene with real-world physical properties, our approach significantly reduces appearance discrepancies and improves generalization under diverse lighting conditions. Additionally, we apply domain randomization across material properties, lighting environments, and object poses to enhance dataset diversity and model robustness.

We validate our synthetic dataset by training a YOLOv5-6D network and evaluating its performance on real metallic parts under diverse and challenging lighting conditions. Despite being trained solely on synthetic data, results indicate that our approach reduces appearance discrepancies and improves pose estimation accuracy in real-world industrial settings.
